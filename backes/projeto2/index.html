Problema de Pesquisa:

Mortalidade Materna em Países em Desenvolvimento

A mortalidade materna continua a ser um desafio significativo em muitos países em desenvolvimento, representando um indicador crítico da saúde pública e da qualidade dos serviços de saúde. Segundo a Organização Mundial da Saúde (OMS), cerca de 295.000 mulheres morreram durante a gravidez e o parto em 2017, com a maioria dessas mortes ocorrendo em países de baixa e média renda (WHO, 2019). Fatores como falta de acesso a cuidados pré-natais adequados, condições socioeconômicas desfavoráveis, desigualdade de gênero e práticas culturais prejudiciais contribuem para a alta mortalidade materna.

O objetivo principal desta pesquisa é investigar os determinantes sociais e estruturais que afetam a saúde materna e a mortalidade associada em contextos de vulnerabilidade. A pesquisa visa identificar intervenções eficazes que possam ser implementadas para reduzir esses índices e promover um acesso equitativo aos serviços de saúde materna.

Introduction and Justification

Maternal mortality remains a serious public health problem, especially in developing countries, where lack of access to health care and structural inequalities increase the risk of complications and deaths during pregnancy and childbirth. In 2017, WHO reported around 295,000 maternal deaths, the majority in low- and middle-income countries. These figures reflect the urgent need to address both the direct causes and the social and structural determinants that influence maternal health, such as access to health care, unfavorable socioeconomic conditions and gender inequality. This mortality is not only the result of inadequate medical care, but also of social, economic and cultural factors. Women in vulnerable situations, with limited access to education and health services, are particularly exposed to these risks. This research aims to investigate how these factors impact maternal health and identify effective interventions to promote equitable access to maternal health services. Maternal mortality is a reflection of social inequality and failures in health systems, especially in developing countries. This study is relevant because it offers a deeper understanding of the social and structural determinants of maternal mortality and provides essential data to improve the quality of health services and reduce existing disparities.

Research Problem:

Maternal Mortality in Developing Countries

Maternal mortality continues to be a significant challenge in many developing countries, representing a critical indicator of public health and the quality of health services. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), an estimated 295,000 women died during pregnancy and childbirth in 2017, with the majority of these deaths occurring in low- and middle-income countries (WHO, 2019). Factors such as lack of access to adequate antenatal care, unfavorable socioeconomic conditions, gender inequality, and harmful cultural practices contribute to high maternal mortality. .

Main Objective:

The main objective of this research is to investigate the social and structural determinants that affect maternal health and associated mortality in vulnerable contexts. The research aims to identify effective interventions that can be implemented to reduce these indices and promote equitable access to maternal health services.

Hypothesis

Social, economic and gender inequalities have a significant impact on maternal mortality in developing countries, with limited access to health services, poor education and gender discrimination increasing the risk of complications during pregnancy and childbirth.
